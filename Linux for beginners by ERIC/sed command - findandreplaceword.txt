sed command work like find and replace in windows. you can do delete,overwrite etc in files.  

========
:~$ cat orange.txt
orange is citrus family food and act like medicine to reduce heat in the body.

:~$  sed 's/family/friend/' orange.txt
orange is citrus friend food and act like medicine to reduce heat in the body.

This replaces word family with friend. In each line of file. It did not overwrited file.
========

-- how to overwrite the file -- aka find and replace

just add -i after sed command.  
syntax - 
  sed -i 's/oldword/newword' filename

=====
sed -i 's/family/friend/' orange.txt
